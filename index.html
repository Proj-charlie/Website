Up-keeping the performance and stability of a Minecraft server requires great effort from administrators. Everyday they ensure that add-ons work, whilst simultaneously chasing after bugs and inconsistencies. These responsibilities take away precious time from what they could be doing, such as is growing their server and focusing on their community.

With every upcoming Minecraft update, performance starts to degrade on a production server. This allows for an almost continuous cycle of chasing performance and stability issues.

In our own adventures, we faced the same problems. After spending countless hours working on patches, we quickly realized the importance of delegation. If we spent less time on chores, we would be able to refocus that time on growing the server and giving our players what they want, new features or events. We aim to take up some of those tasks so you can do something else.
